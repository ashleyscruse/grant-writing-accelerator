# Grant Types

The same project gets written four different ways depending on who is funding it. Tone, structure, length, and the standard of evidence all shift with the funder type. A proposal that scores well with a federal agency reads as cold and overlong to a foundation program officer, and a foundation narrative reads as thin and unscored to a federal review panel.

Before you draft, know which type you are writing for, and tell the AI. That single fact changes how every section gets built.

## Comparison at a Glance

| Dimension | Federal | Foundation | Corporate | Congressional / CPF |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Goes to | A federal agency review panel | A foundation board and program officers | A company's CSR or giving team | A Senator or House member, not an agency |
| Driving logic | Scored rubric, public policy benefit | Mission alignment and story | Mutual benefit and brand fit | Community support and local impact |
| Tone | Formal, prescriptive | Conversational but precise | Concise, business-oriented | Formal, readiness-focused |
| Length | Long, strict page limits | Shorter (often 5 to 15 pages) | Short (often 2 to 5 pages) | Detailed project description plus documentation |
| Evidence standard | Heavy: citations, validated instruments, statistics | Data supports the story | Clear deliverables and reporting | Cost estimates plus community support docs |
| Timeline to decision | 6 to 12 months | 2 to 6 months | Faster, smaller amounts | Tied to the appropriations calendar |
| Distinctive demand | Mirror the RFP, address every criterion | Advance the funder's theory of change | Recognition and visibility | Letters, resolutions, federal authorization tie |

## Federal

Federal grants are highly structured and prescriptive: strict formats, page limits, required forms, and a points-based review by expert panels who score sections independently. They are data-driven and evidence-based, and they take 80 to 100+ hours to prepare. Because they run on taxpayer dollars, they must demonstrate public benefit and align with federal policy.

Write to the scoring rubric. Mirror the language of the RFP, RFA, or NOFO exactly, use section headers that match the evaluation criteria, address every criterion in order with explicit language, and back every claim with peer-reviewed research or government data and validated instruments. Assume each reviewer scores your sections without reading the others, so each section must stand on its own.

For NIH proposals, note the simplified peer review framework (effective January 2025): three factors replace the former five. Factor 1 is Importance of the Research (significance plus innovation), Factor 2 is Rigor and Feasibility (the approach, which correlates most strongly with the overall score), and Factor 3 is Expertise and Resources, evaluated as sufficient or not.

## Foundation

Foundation grants are narrative-driven and relationship-centered. Proposals are shorter and more flexible, review is holistic, and decisions come faster (2 to 6 months). Budgets are more flexible, and the sector is trending toward general operating support and trust-based philanthropy.

Lead with story and mission alignment, supported by data rather than buried in it. Use the foundation's own language, reference its strategic priorities, and show how your project advances its specific theory of change. The tone is more conversational but still precise and evidence-supported. Before you write, research the foundation's giving history, board, and strategic plan, and if it accepts letters of inquiry, use an LOI to test fit before investing in a full proposal.

## Corporate

Corporate grants are tied to corporate social responsibility and brand strategy. They are short (often 2 to 5 pages), turn around quickly, and tend to be smaller. They usually want visibility and recognition, and they favor projects within the company's geographic footprint or strategic interest areas.

Emphasize mutual benefit: how the project advances the company's CSR goals, what recognition and visibility it offers (naming, sponsorship, logo placement), and what employee engagement is possible (volunteering, skills-based partnerships, matching gifts). Keep it concise and business-oriented, use the company's CSR language, and state clear deliverables and reporting commitments.

## Congressional / Community Project Funding

Congressional Directed Spending, also called Community Project Funding, goes to a Senator or House member rather than to an agency. Competition is intense (House members are capped at 15 requests each), and community support documentation is mandatory. Projects must tie to existing federal authorization law and eligible program accounts, and no for-profit recipients are allowed. Preparation should begin 6 to 12 months ahead of the constituent submission window, which typically falls in January and February.

Document broad community support through diverse letters and resolutions from local governing bodies. Tie the project to specific federal program accounts and authorization language, show financial and administrative readiness equal to a competitive federal grant, and emphasize local economic impact, job creation, and community benefit. Align the request with the member's stated priorities and district needs.

## Telling the AI the Type

The AI applies the right conventions only when you name the type in the prompt. State it plainly at the top, and add the funder name and the specific priorities you want mirrored.

```
This is a FEDERAL proposal for NSF.
Mirror the NOFO language exactly and use the evaluation criteria as
section headers. Score-aware: each reviewer reads sections independently.
Back every claim with a citation or validated instrument.
```

```
This is a FOUNDATION proposal for the [name] Foundation.
Lead with mission alignment and story, supported by data.
Reference their stated priority on [paste priority] and their theory
of change. Keep it under 10 pages and conversational but precise.
```

```
This is a CONGRESSIONAL / Community Project Funding request to
[member name].
Emphasize local economic impact and community support. Tie the project
to [federal program account / authorization]. Keep tone formal and
readiness-focused; we will attach letters and resolutions.
```

When in doubt about which type you are facing, check the solicitation: a scored rubric and required forms signal federal, a theory of change and an LOI option signal foundation, a CSR or giving page signals corporate, and a constituent submission window signals congressional.
